# S-06: Accessibility Audit & Inclusion Improvement Drive on Campus

## Social Challenge
**People with disabilities — mobility impairments, visual impairments, hearing impairments — often face barriers in university campus infrastructure, digital resources, and classroom environments that exclude them from full participation.**

## Background
Inclusion requires proactive evaluation. An audit of physical and digital campus features enables structured improvements that comply with national guidelines and foster an equitable environment.

## Project Objectives
1. Conduct a comprehensive accessibility audit of campus infrastructure and digital assets
2. Benchmark findings against RPWD Act 2016 and WCAG 2.1 standards
3. Produce a prioritized remediation roadmap for university administration
4. Engage students with disabilities in participatory feedback sessions
5. Run an awareness campaign to build an inclusive campus culture
6. Pilot at least one low-cost accessibility improvement with admin support

## Stakeholders
| Stakeholder | Role |
| :--- | :--- |
| Students/staff with disabilities | Central voice and co-researchers |
| University facilities management | Implementation partner |
| University administration | Decision makers |
| Disability rights NGOs | Advisory input |
| General student body | Awareness audience |

## Requirements
- **Research**: Study RPWD Act 2016 accessibility mandates; WCAG 2.1 AA checklist; international campus accessibility standards.
- **Survey**: Campus accessibility experience survey targeting students/staff with disabilities (qualitative + quantitative).
- **Execution**: Physical audit (ramps, signage, washrooms, labs, library); digital audit (university website, LMS); participatory sessions; advocacy document.
- **Documentation**: Audit checklists, photo evidence, survey data, remediation roadmap, advocacy report.
